Current Projects

My current research focuses on modeling human behavior through intention prediction using egocentric data. We are working with the Aria glasses, a wearable device that captures rich multimodal information from a first-person aka human point of view. In addition to leveraging existing data, we aim to create our own dataset that captures everday routines and allows us to identify meaningful behavioral patterns from it. Our goal is to develop explainable AI systems that can infer intentions in dynamic environments, bridging the gap between open-world perception and closed-world reasoning.

Our work has potential applications in robotics, assistive technologies, wearable tech, and human-AI collaboration, especially in real-world settings where anticipating human actions is critical for safe and intelligent interactions.
